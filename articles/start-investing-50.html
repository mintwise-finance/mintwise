How to Start Investing With $50 or Less

Mar 18, 2026 8 min read

The biggest myth in personal finance is that you need thousands of dollars to start investing. Ten years ago, that might have been true because brokers charged $10 per trade. Today, trading is free, and barriers to entry have been completely destroyed.

The Magic of Fractional Shares

If a single share of Amazon costs around $180, and you only have $50, how do you invest? The answer is fractional shares. Nearly all modern brokerage apps (Fidelity, Charles Schwab, Robinhood) allow you to buy "slices" of a share. You simply type in $50, and you will own roughly 0.27 shares of Amazon.

Forget Picking Individual Stocks

If you're starting with $50, trying to pick the "next Apple" is practically gambling. Professional wall street traders fail to beat the market 85% of the time, so you shouldn't try either.

Instead, invest in Index Funds or ETFs. An Index Fund is a basket containing hundreds of stocks. When you buy an S&P 500 ETF (like VOO or SPY), your $50 is instantly divided and invested into the 500 largest companies in America. If one company fails, you don't lose your money because you still own 499 others.

Robo-Advisors Make it Effortless

If you don't even want to pick an ETF, you can use a Robo-Advisor like Betterment or Wealthfront. You answer 5 simple questions about your goals, deposit your $50, and an algorithm builds and manages a diversified portfolio for you automatically. They charge a tiny fee (around 0.25% per year) for this convenience.

Why Starting Small Matters

Investing $50 today won't make you a millionaire tomorrow. But it builds the habit of investing. Getting over the psychological hurdle of making your very first investment is the hardest part. Once you see your $50 turn into $52, you'll find ways to invest $100 next month.
